# Achieving Low Latency for Multipath Transmission in RDMA Based Data Center Network

## Overview

RDMA struggles in heterogeneous multipath environments due to its sensitivity to packet reordering, leading to packet blocking and excessive buffering at the receiver’s NIC. To address these challenges, this paper proposes an advanced fast retransmission multipath RDMA scheme (AFR-MPRDMA) that proactively detects congestion and reroutes blocked packets through the least congested available paths, optimizing transmission efficiency. The proposed mechanism reduces latency and packet loss by dynamically adjusting congestion control and path selection. Evaluation results demonstrate that AFR-MPRDMA significantly enhances RDMA performance, making it more robust in multipath data center networks.

## Objective

The paper proposes AFR-MPRDMA, a novel RDMA-based multipath transmission scheme that improves end-to-end latency and network efficiency by dynamically retransmitting blocked packets on alternative faster paths, thereby reducing buffering delays and minimizing packet reordering issues.

## Background

RDMA, Multipath Transmission, DCN, Congestion Control, Fast Retransmission Mechanisms

## As-Is

- RDMA achieves high performance by offloading transport logic to the NIC but struggles with multipath environments due to packet reordering sensitivity.

- Existing RDMA congestion control mechanisms lack efficient out-of-order packet handling, leading to prolonged buffering at the receiver’s NIC and degraded transmission performance.

- Current multipath transmission techniques, such as Equal-Cost Multipath Routing (ECMP), fail to optimize for RDMA workloads as they rely on static hash-based traffic distribution and lack real-time congestion awareness, preventing dynamic path adjustments.

- Without a robust and adaptive retransmission mechanism, RDMA networks suffer from increased packet drops and inefficient data flow, negatively impacting performance.

## To-Be

- AFR-MPRDMA introduces an advanced fast retransmission mechanism that detects slow-path congestion by continuously monitoring packet arrival times, queuing delays, and acknowledgment feedback from the receiver, then retransmits affected packets on alternative faster paths based on real-time congestion metrics.

- AFR-MPRDMA enhances RDMA’s multipath capability by utilizing software-based congestion detection and adaptive retransmission mechanisms, ensuring efficient deployment without significant modifications to existing hardware infrastructure.

- The scheme leverages a receiver-side packet management system that actively monitors packet sequence numbers, detects out-of-order arrivals, and applies intelligent reordering algorithms to minimize buffering requirements and improve data retrieval efficiency.

- Unlike traditional RDMA congestion control techniques, AFR-MPRDMA enables proactive packet retransmission, reducing the risk of prolonged transmission delays.

## Methodology

- The proposed AFR-MPRDMA scheme is evaluated using RDMA-enabled data center network simulations.

- The authors implement AFR-MPRDMA within an RDMA simulation framework to measure its impact on latency, throughput, and congestion control.

- Performance comparisons are made against conventional RDMA multipath techniques such as ECMP, DCQCN (Data Center Quantized Congestion Notification), TIMELY (TCP Incast Mitigation by Early Retransmission), and existing retransmission-based approaches like RACK (Recent Acknowledgment).

- The study assesses the effectiveness of AFR-MPRDMA under different network congestion scenarios, ensuring robustness across diverse conditions.

- Key performance metrics include end-to-end latency, packet reordering rates, and receiver buffer utilization.

## Results

- AFR-MPRDMA reduces RDMA packet transmission latency by 32% by dynamically adjusting retransmission paths.

- The scheme significantly decreases packet blocking occurrences by 40% and enhances overall data transmission efficiency.

- Evaluation results demonstrate a 25% improvement in throughput and a 30% reduction in receiver NIC buffering overhead.

- The proposed method shows superior adaptability to varying congestion levels compared to traditional multipath RDMA approaches, achieving up to 35% better congestion mitigation.

- Future research directions include exploring machine learning-based congestion prediction techniques to further enhance AFR-MPRDMA’s decision-making capabilities.
