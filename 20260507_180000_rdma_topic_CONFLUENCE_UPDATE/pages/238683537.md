# Multi-Path Transport for RDMA in Datacenters

<!-- id=238683537 parent=238780418 -->

## Overview

This paper introduces MP-RDMA, a novel multipath transport solution for Remote Direct Memory Access (RDMA) in datacenters. RDMA provides low-latency, high-throughput communication but struggles with congestion and load balancing in multipath environments. Traditional TCP-based multipath solutions are not directly applicable to RDMA due to its lack of retransmissions and in-order packet delivery requirement. MP-RDMA proposes a per-packet adaptive routing mechanism that utilizes real-time Explicit Congestion Notification (ECN) feedback and dynamically selects paths based on congestion levels to maximize throughput and ensure fairness across flows. Extensive evaluations demonstrate MP-RDMA's ability to outperform existing RDMA transport mechanisms, significantly improving throughput and tail latency while maintaining fairness across multiple flows.

## Objective

The objective of this paper is to design MP-RDMA, a multipath transport mechanism for RDMA in datacenters that enhances network throughput, reduces tail latency, and ensures fair bandwidth distribution while remaining compatible with existing RDMA hardware.

## Background

RDMA, Congestion Control, Multipath Transport, ECN, Datacenter Networks

## As-Is

- Existing RDMA transports, such as RoCE, rely on Priority-based Flow Control (PFC) for congestion management, which can cause deadlocks and head-of-line blocking.

- Multipath transport can improve RDMA performance by utilizing multiple paths, but traditional solutions are TCP-based and not directly applicable to RDMA due to its requirements for in-order delivery and lack of retransmissions.

- ECN-based congestion control, such as DCQCN, helps mitigate congestion by marking packets based on queue occupancy, but it does not fully leverage multipath capabilities due to its reliance on end-to-end congestion signals rather than real-time path diversity utilization.

- Existing RDMA mechanisms lack efficient load balancing across multiple paths, leading to suboptimal throughput and unfair resource allocation.

## To-Be

- MP-RDMA introduces per-packet adaptive routing to address the inefficiencies of existing RDMA transports, which suffer from congestion and lack of efficient load balancing. By enabling real-time congestion-aware path selection, MP-RDMA mitigates the limitations of static path selection and TCP-based multipath solutions, ensuring improved throughput and fairness across multiple flows.

- A central component of MP-RDMA is a lightweight congestion estimator that ensures load balancing while preserving RDMA's in-order delivery requirement.

- Persistent queue buildup occurs when network buffers continuously accumulate packets due to sustained congestion, leading to increased queuing delays and potential packet drops. MP-RDMA avoids this issue by dynamically selecting paths with lower congestion, preventing prolonged queuing and network hotspots.

## Methodology

- Implementation of MP-RDMA on a real RDMA hardware setup with software modifications.

- Use of a testbed with Mellanox NICs and a multi-path topology to validate performance.

- Comparison against DCQCN-based congestion control mechanisms.

- Measurement of throughput, tail latency, and fairness using synthetic workloads and real-world datacenter traffic patterns.

- Simulation of large-scale datacenter scenarios to assess MP-RDMA's scalability.

## Results

- MP-RDMA improves throughput by up to 40% compared to traditional single-path RDMA transport. It reduces tail latency by up to 2.6x under heavy network congestion.

- The mechanism ensures fair bandwidth allocation across multiple RDMA flows. MP-RDMA effectively mitigates persistent queue buildup and reduces congestion hotspots.

- Future work includes exploring hardware-level optimizations for even lower latency and investigating adaptive congestion control mechanisms for heterogeneous datacenter environments.
