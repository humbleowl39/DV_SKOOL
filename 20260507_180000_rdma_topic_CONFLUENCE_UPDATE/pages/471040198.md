# 3. Design a calculator engine

<!-- id=471040198 parent=471040007 -->

## Introduction

In this step, you will design a FPGA-based arithmetic calculator engine. The engine receives two operands and add them. You can verify the result by reading the result from the engine. The result shall be as follows.

### Input

<div>

<div>

./my_calculator 13 21

</div>

</div>

### Output

<div>

<div>

\$ dmesg ... \[33517.137631\] qdma_driver: write 13 21 \[33517.137631\] qdma_driver: read 34

</div>

</div>

## Hint

### AXI

AXI (Advanced eXtensible Interface) is an on-chip communication bus protocol to connect two different modules. QDMA uses AXI-Lite protocol to communicate with other modules. Please refer to the materials at the link below or other online resources
